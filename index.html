01 — About

Who I Am

I am a PhD student and Graduate Teaching & Research Assistant at Oklahoma State University's Boone Pickens School of Geology. My work sits at the intersection of geomechanics and computational science — I study how fluids move through porous geological media, how rocks and soils deform under stress, and how we can model these processes accurately using numerical methods and machine learning.

My background spans soil and rock mechanics, 3D microstructure modeling, Micro-CT pore analysis, and deep learning applications for geoscience problems. I am a member of the Society of Exploration Geophysicists (SEG) and the American Institute of Professional Geologists (AIPG).

Originally from Sri Lanka, I bring a project-driven approach to research — I care deeply about building things that work, not just writing about them.

02 — Research

Research & Publications

Current Research Focus

◕

Porous Media Flow Simulation

3D pore microstructure modeling and fluid flow simulation in stochastically generated pore networks. Involves conversion of MATLAB-based stochastic models to Python and meshing with Star CCM+ for CFD analysis.

⊕

Fractional Reservoir Equation (FRE) Modeling

Numerical PDE solving for anomalous diffusion in geological reservoirs using fractional calculus frameworks. Python-based implementation with high-resolution spatial and temporal plotting.

■

Micro-CT Pore Analysis

ImageJ/Fiji-based quantitative analysis of pore size distributions from micro-CT scan data. Characterizing pore geometry for input into flow simulation models.

◆

ML for Geothermal Resource Identification

Applying supervised and deep learning methods to the GDR Submission 616 dataset for geothermal prospectivity mapping and resource classification.
